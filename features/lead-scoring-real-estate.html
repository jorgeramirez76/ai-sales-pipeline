Not All Leads Are Created Equal

You know this intuitively. Some leads are ready to move next week. Others are "just looking" and won't buy for 18 months. The problem? Most agents treat them the same — wasting precious time on leads that will never convert while hot prospects cool off.

AI Lead Scoring changes the game. Our machine learning model analyzes dozens of signals in real-time to predict which leads are most likely to transact — and when. It's like having a crystal ball that tells you exactly where to focus your energy.

The AI doesn't just look at what leads tell you. It analyzes their behavior — which listings they view, how often they return, what they click, when they're active, and dozens of other signals. It knows the difference between a casual browser and a motivated buyer before they even realize it themselves.

How Lead Scoring Works

🔥 Hot/Warm/Cold

Every lead gets a temperature score. Hot leads need immediate attention. Warm leads need nurturing. Cold leads get long-term follow-up.

📊 Behavioral Analysis

The AI tracks page views, time on site, property saves, price alerts, return visits, and more to gauge intent.

💬 Engagement Scoring

Email opens, SMS replies, call answers — every interaction updates the score. High engagement = high priority.

⏰ Timeline Prediction

The AI estimates when a lead is likely to transact — "Ready now," "3 months," "6+ months" — so you can plan accordingly.

🎯 Automated Routing

Hot leads trigger immediate alerts and fast-track workflows. Your best opportunities never sit in a queue.

📈 Constant Learning

The model improves over time, learning from your actual closed deals to get even more accurate.

What The AI Considers

Demographics: Location, price range, property type preferences, financing status

Behavior: Search frequency, listing views, time on site, saved properties, price changes viewed

Engagement: Email opens, click-through rates, SMS responses, call interactions

Source: Organic search, paid ads, referrals, Zillow, Facebook — each scored differently

Timing: Days since last activity, response speed, seasonal patterns
