The Jab-Jab-Jab-Right-Hook framework

Gary Vaynerchuk's book Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook (2013) argues that marketers and salespeople ruin their relationships by asking for the sale too quickly. His alternative: three 'jabs' (genuine value, no ask) for every 'right hook' (the ask). In sales terms: three messages where you help, educate, or entertain the prospect with no commercial intent, then one message where you make the offer. The 3:1 ratio creates permission. The prospect feels like you've been useful to them, so when you finally pitch, they lean in rather than resist.

How your Custom AI Brain applies it

The AI runs your outreach on a 3:1 cadence. For a real estate lead, that might look like: Message 1 — market data snapshot for their neighborhood (jab). Message 2 — just-sold alert for a comparable home (jab). Message 3 — short educational tip on pricing strategy (jab). Message 4 — offer to do a free valuation (right hook). Most salespeople jump to message 4 immediately. GaryVee-trained AI doesn't. The AI never sends an ask without having earned it through prior value.

Document, don't create

GaryVee's companion principle: the fastest way to build content and trust is to document what you're already doing, not invent new content from scratch. The AI applies this to outreach: instead of writing generic 'thinking of you' messages, it references the specifics of your actual business. 'Just listed a property in Summit — if you know anyone in the market, here's the link.' Documentary tone beats marketing tone every time.

Authentic hustle without burnout

GaryVee's public philosophy: work ethic matters more than talent. But there's a limit to human work ethic. The AI extends the agent's authentic voice into 24/7 outreach without demanding 24/7 work. The agent records their actual thinking on key topics; the AI delivers it as situationally-appropriate. Nobody can write 50 personalized messages a day forever. AI can.

Frequently asked questions

Is this endorsed by Gary Vaynerchuk or VaynerMedia?

No — we're not affiliated with Gary Vaynerchuk or VaynerMedia. We've built our AI to apply his publicly-taught content and sales methodology (from his books, keynotes, and GaryVee content across social) because it works. Think 'AI that follows the GaryVee playbook' — not 'AI Gary.'

Doesn't the 3-jab ratio slow down sales cycles?

Counterintuitively, no. It speeds up close rate. The 3-jab approach lowers prospect resistance — so when the right hook comes, closes happen faster than cold pitches would. For short-cycle industries (auto, retail), we compress the ratio to 2:1 or 1:1; for relationship industries (real estate, wealth management, B2B), 3:1 is ideal.

How does the AI determine what counts as a 'jab'?

Anything that provides value with no commercial intent. Educational content (tips, data, explanations). Entertainment (appropriate humor, storytelling). Helpful resources (tools, links, introductions). Recognition (congratulations on milestones, birthdays). The AI is trained to pattern-match to your actual business — real estate agents jab with market data and just-sold alerts; advisors jab with tax deadline reminders and educational content.

Does this work for cold outreach or only warm?

Both, but the jabs are different. Warm outreach jabs reference existing relationship ('your home's value trajectory this year'). Cold outreach jabs start with public information ('just saw a Zillow valuation on your block — did you see this?') — enough to open the door without being creepy. The first cold jab is the hardest to calibrate; we usually go through 2-3 iterations to land the right tone.

Is authentic-voice AI a contradiction in terms?

It can be, if done poorly. Well-trained voice-cloning AI preserves the actual phrases, rhythms, and pet topics of the human it's modeled on. A GaryVee-trained AI doesn't try to be GaryVee; it applies GaryVee's principles while sounding like YOU. The authenticity is in the matching of your actual voice, not in faking someone else's.

Does this combine with Hormozi or Cardone?

Yes — different layers. GaryVee's philosophy is about relationship and permission building (the long game). Hormozi is offer construction. Cardone is volume and persistence. Many of our best configurations: GaryVee's jab cadence for the relationship build + Hormozi offers at the right-hook moments + Cardone-level follow-up persistence. All three compatible, all three layered into one AI persona.
