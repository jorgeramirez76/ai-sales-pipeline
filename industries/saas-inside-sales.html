Frequently asked questions

How does AI compare to Chili Piper or Qualified?

Those tools solve one piece (demo booking, chatbot qualification). AI Sales Pipeline is the end-to-end — inbound qualification, routing, demo booking, no-show recovery, post-demo follow-up, trial activation. You can use AI Sales Pipeline instead of multiple point tools, or integrate alongside Chili Piper for companies that have it deployed already.

Does AI replace SDRs?

Not fully — but it meaningfully changes the SDR role. AI handles 80% of inbound qualification (the repetitive BANT stuff). SDRs shift to outbound prospecting, complex multi-thread deals, and strategic account plays. Most SaaS teams using AI reduce SDR headcount 20-30% while increasing pipeline quality.

What about ABM (account-based marketing) coordination?

AI integrates with ABM tools (Demandbase, 6sense) to pattern-match inbound against target account lists. When a lead arrives from a target account, AI priority-routes to the named AE and flags marketing for coordinated touches.

Can AI handle the free-trial to paid conversion?

Yes — and this is where most SaaS teams leave the most money on the table. AI runs in-trial engagement (usage alerts, feature education, on-boarding reminders), identifies trial users about to convert vs churn, and handles the upgrade conversation. Typical lift: 15-25% increase in trial-to-paid conversion.

Does AI work for product-led growth (PLG) SaaS?

Yes — with different workflows. PLG SaaS focuses on in-product activation, not demo booking. AI handles activation nudges (user hasn't invited teammates after 3 days, user hasn't set up integration X), which is where PLG conversion lives. The SDR-style workflows are less relevant; the product-qualified-lead (PQL) workflows are.

How does AI handle the security questions on initial demo requests?

AI answers publicly-disclosable security info (SOC 2 status, ISO certifications, hosting regions) without routing to human. Deeper questions (custom DPA, red-team results, specific data residency) route to the security team. This saves AEs from getting dragged into security Q&A early in the cycle.
